Anguilla Summer Festival 2026 —
The Complete Guide

July 17, 2026 · Roam Anguilla Team · 12 min read
🎭🎉🌴

The Anguilla Summer Festival is back, and this year it's shaping up to be one for the history books. From the electric chaos of J'Ouvert morning to the elegance of the Miss Anguilla Pageant, the thunder of boat racing to the pure bacchanal of the Caribbean Beach Party — this is the island's biggest celebration of culture, community, and pure Caribbean joy. And here's the best part: prices this year are incredibly affordable, making it the most accessible festival in recent memory. Turnout is expected to be massive. If there was ever a year to experience Anguilla Carnival, this is it.

Whether you're a first-timer trying to figure out what "J'Ouvert" means or a seasoned Carnival veteran mapping out your fete schedule, we've put together everything you need to know. Below you'll find the full schedule, detailed breakdowns of every major event, pricing information, and insider tips to help you navigate the festival like a local.

🎤 The Calypso Competitions

Late July EC$25

🇦🇮 Senior Calypso Semi-Finals

The best of the best in local and regional acts compete in this classic competition grounded in the region's deep musical identity. Calypso isn't just music in the Caribbean — it's storytelling, social commentary, and cultural preservation wrapped in infectious rhythm. Former contenders go head-to-head to win the crown in both male and female categories, with each performance judged on lyrics, melody, stage presence, and crowd response. The semi-finals are where legends are made and dreams are broken — expect sharp wordplay, political satire, and melodies that'll stick with you long after the tent clears. If you've never experienced live Calypso, prepare to be converted.

Late July EC$35

🏆 Leeward Islands Calypso Monarch

The stakes go regional. Winners from Anguilla and neighboring Leeward Islands face off in a battle for Caribbean Calypso supremacy. This is where you'll hear the finest calypsonians from across the Eastern Caribbean delivering their sharpest material. The competition is fierce, the wordplay is razor-sharp, and the atmosphere is electric. A must-see for anyone who appreciates music with meaning.

👑 The Pageants — Elegance, Grace & Community Pride

Late July / Early August EC$40

👸🏾 Miss Anguilla Pageant

The crème de la crème of Summer Festival events. This prestigious two-part competition takes place over consecutive evenings, and it's the crown jewel of Anguilla's cultural calendar. The fairest and strongest representations of Anguilla's beauty and intellect showcase their talents, poise, and advocacy on their local stage. Each contestant presents in multiple categories — evening wear, talent, interview, and swimwear — all curated to test the depth of character as much as outward grace. Fans come out in droves to support their favorite queen, transforming the venue into a sea of banners, cowbells, and passionate cheers. Guaranteed to be two nights of elegance, fierce competition, and the kind of creative expression that stays with you. This is where Anguilla crowns its ambassador to the world.

Late July EC$20

👶🏾 Prince & Princess Show · Miss Talented Teen · Miss Tiny Tott

The youth pageants are where the community's heart truly shines. From the adorable Prince & Princess Show featuring the island's littlest stars, to Miss Talented Teen where young women demonstrate remarkable poise and ambition, to the charming Miss Tiny Tott — these events celebrate Anguilla's next generation. Proud parents, extended families, and entire neighborhoods turn out in support, making these some of the most genuinely heartwarming events of the festival. Great for families and anyone who wants to see the community spirit that makes Anguilla special.

🎺 Band-O-Rama — The Ultimate Soca Showdown

Early August EC$35

🎵 Band Clash / Band-O-Rama

The top-notch night where local and regional soca bands go head-to-head on an international stage for a night of complete bacchanal. If you're a fan of soca music, you genuinely do not want to miss this. Each band brings their full arsenal — brass sections wailing, rhythm sections thundering, lead vocalists commanding the crowd — competing for bragging rights and the roar of thousands. The energy builds throughout the night as bands trade sets, each trying to outdo the last with bigger drops, tighter arrangements, and more crowd participation. It gathers quite the crowd, and by the end of the night, the distinction between performer and audience completely dissolves. Pure, unfiltered Caribbean joy.

🇦🇮 August Monday (August 3rd) — The Main Event

Monday Aug 3 · 5:00 AM – 12:00 PM FREE

🌅 J'Ouvert Morning

J'Ouvert (from the French jour ouvert, meaning "daybreak") is a long-loved Caribbean tradition, and in Anguilla it's nothing short of legendary. What starts as a small crowd gathering in the cool, dark hours of early morning in The Valley soon becomes a swelling sea of fun-loving party seekers filling the streets with laughter, music, and dancing for miles. The massive street party follows a time-honored route from The Valley down to Sandy Ground, with DJ trucks, live steel pan, and mobile sound systems keeping the energy sky-high. Revellers — covered in paint, mud, chocolate, or just their finest carnival attire — move as one pulsating organism of celebration.

The beauty of J'Ouvert is its freedom: feel free to hop in and hop out along the path whenever you like. Found yourself tired at 8 AM? Step aside, grab a local breakfast from a roadside vendor, and jump back in at 10. The party flows with you. Caution: Traffic along the route can be genuinely tricky — roads close early, parking fills fast, and navigating between points requires patience. Plan in advance, arrange a designated driver or taxi, and embrace the beautiful chaos.

Monday Aug 3 · Afternoon FREE

🚤 Sir Emile Gumbs Boat Race

On the afternoon of August Monday, the action shifts to the waters of Sandy Ground for one of Anguilla's most cherished traditions. Named in honour of the godfather of boat racing in Anguilla, Sir Emile Gumbs, this race transforms Sandy Ground into a vibrant festival-within-a-festival. Former J'Ouvert morning revellers — now showered, refreshed, and ready for round two — line the beach alongside die-hard boat racing enthusiasts cheering on their favourite teams. The wooden boats, hand-built by local craftsmen and maintained with religious devotion, cut through the Caribbean waters at breathtaking speeds.

Between races, the beach becomes a cultural marketplace: local food vendors serve up grilled lobster, roasted corn, johnny cakes, and ice-cold drinks; families stake out their spots under colourful umbrellas; and the air fills with the smell of barbecue and the sound of reggae drifting from portable speakers. It's the perfect pre-cursor for the evening activities to come — a uniquely Anguillian blend of sport, community, and seaside relaxation.

Monday Aug 3 · Afternoon – Late Night EC$60

🏖️ Caribbean Beach Party

The ultimate beach party in the Caribbean. As the afternoon sun begins its descent over Sandy Ground Beach, crowds in the thousands gather on the shore for what has become the defining event of the Anguilla Summer Festival. The Caribbean Beach Party is exactly what it sounds like and so much more — a massive, open-air celebration where the sand becomes your dance floor and the stars become your ceiling.

The performances and party actually begin in the afternoon, with local DJs and warm-up acts setting the vibe while the crowd settles in. Food stalls line the perimeter serving everything from jerk chicken to lobster tails, and the bars flow freely. But when night falls, the energy shifts into another gear entirely. Star-studded performances from acts around the world take the stage — soca superstars, dancehall icons, reggae legends, and international DJs — delivering explosive sets that shake the sand beneath your feet. Revellers usually party and soak in the atmosphere for hours, with the music often continuing well into the early morning. The combination of world-class performances, the Caribbean Sea at your feet, and thousands of people united in pure celebration makes this a bucket-list experience. If you attend only one event during the Summer Festival, make it this one.

🎭 More Festival Highlights

Early August FREE

🎨 Grand Parade of Troupes

Across the Caribbean, Carnival culminates in the Grand Parade — the spectacular procession of costumed masquerade bands, or "troupes," through the streets in a kaleidoscope of colour, feathers, sequins, and sound. Anguilla's Grand Parade of Troupes follows this proud tradition with its own island flair. Thousands of revellers in elaborate, hand-crafted costumes — each section telling its own story through colour and design — dance through the streets to the relentless pulse of soca music pumped from massive speaker trucks. Months of preparation, costume-building, and choreography culminate in this single, glorious day of mas. Whether you choose to "play mas" with a troupe or watch from the sidelines with a cold drink in hand, the Grand Parade is Carnival in its purest, most visual form — a moving museum of Caribbean creativity that you simply cannot look away from.

Early August Varies

🚀 Poker Run

Imagine this: dozens of high-powered speedboats lined up on the shoreline, engines roaring, as crews prepare for a massive, all-day race around the entire island of Anguilla. The Poker Run is part race, part scavenger hunt, and completely exhilarating. Boats tear across the turquoise waters, stopping at designated beaches around the island where each stop brings its own unique challenge — and an opportunity to win cold hard cash. Thousands of spectators gather at key vantage points along the coast to watch the spectacle unfold. The combination of raw horsepower, Caribbean seascapes, and the thrill of the chase makes the Poker Run one of the most unique and adrenaline-filled events on the festival calendar.

Throughout the Festival Varies

🎉 Carnival at Leon's · Fetes & More

Beyond the main stage events, the Summer Festival spawns countless fetes, parties, and pop-up events across the island. Carnival at Leon's has become a staple of the festival circuit — an intimate yet energetic venue where locals and visitors alike gather for premium drinks, great music, and the kind of spontaneous fun that defines Caribbean nightlife. Throughout the festival period, keep your ear to the ground for impromptu street parties, boat cruises, and late-night lime sessions. Some of the best Summer Festival memories happen at the events you didn't plan for.

💡 Why This Year Is Special

If you've been on the fence about experiencing Anguilla Summer Festival, let 2026 be the year you take the plunge. Prices across nearly every event are remarkably affordable this year — we're talking EC$20–60 for premium events that in comparable Caribbean festivals would run you three to four times that. The Anguilla Festivals Development Commission has clearly prioritized accessibility, and the result is a festival lineup that feels generous and welcoming to both locals and visitors.

Early indicators suggest turnout will be among the strongest in recent memory. Hotels and villas are booking up, flights to AXA are filling, and the buzz across social media is palpable. This is shaping up to be a festival for the ages — one where the combination of world-class talent, accessible pricing, and that unmistakable Anguillian warmth creates something genuinely magical.
